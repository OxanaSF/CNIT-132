Standardizing <select>:
What the future holds for HTML Controls

         The meeting that took place at the Google San Francisco Office was mainly devoted to the problems that front-and developers have been facing in their professional life and to the ways to solve the problems. The scheme that the involved people has been using is to collect opinions and improve technologies based on them.

         There was a Program Manager for Developer Experiences on Microsoft Edge who comes from a background in web and user interface design as well as front-end development Stephanie Stimac as a speaker. She began with recap HTML and CSS evolution, reasons wht controls were created. Then she demonstrated statistic of the most requested new naative components among which were <virtual-list>, <toggle-switch>, <input-masks>, edit menue for selcted content, focus trap region, and <segment-button>.

         Stephanie Shtimak noted that a group of professionals under project called The Web We Want who work to improve technologies actively communicate with the dev community and representatives of cool technology companies such as GitHub, Ember, LinkedIn, React, and others. Every member of dev community (all of us, CCSF CS program students are such members as well) is welcome to:

  • Provide feedback on the current HTML Control prototypes that Chrome has
  • Contribute to the form control investigations on Open UI
  • Tell browser vendors what you need from your form controls
